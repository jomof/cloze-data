    "writeup": "**ずにはすまない** is used to indicate that one “has no choice but to (A)”, or “won’t get away without doing (A)”. It is a formal expression that combines the negative classical auxiliary verb **ず**, the case-marking particle **に**, the adverbial particle **は**, and the standard negated form of the verb **済む** (sumu) “to come to pass” or “to finish”.  A more literal translation is “it will not come to pass that (A) is not done,” with the more natural English interpretations conveying the same feeling.

As suggested by the initial **ず**, this expression always follows verbs.  When used with the verb **する** (suru), the correct conjugation is **ぜずにはすまない** (zezu ni wa sumanai).

The standard negative form of a verb (ない form) can also be followed by **ないではすまない** (nai de wa sumanai). This has the same meaning as **ずにはすまない**.

**Important Considerations:**

*   This construction tends to sound unnatural when the speaker considers (A) necessary solely based on their personal feelings. (A) is usually something expected by most people.
