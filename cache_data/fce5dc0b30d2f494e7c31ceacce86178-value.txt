More literally, it suggests \"going ahead with the understanding that (A) is an exception.\" It's used to indicate that a specific point (A) is being disregarded or treated as an exception before discussing the main issue.

    As **は** marks the topic, this phrase can follow a noun or other elements typically used with **は**, including question words like **かどうか** (ka dou ka - whether or not).

    It's common to see the element being set aside (A) presented in both its positive and negative forms, like  「美味しい美味しくないは別として」 (oishii oishikunai wa betsu to shite - whether it's delicious or not). This emphasizes the \"whether or not\" aspect.

    While this grammar point is used to exclude something from the discussion, the reason is often to highlight that the excluded item is an exception to a general rule or consistent factor.

    **Important Consideration:**
