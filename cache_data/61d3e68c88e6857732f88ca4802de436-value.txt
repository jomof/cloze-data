To emphasize the reason, you'll frequently see **んだ** after verbs and i-adjectives, and **なんだ** after na-adjectives and nouns, resulting in **〜んだもの** and **〜なんだもの**, respectively.

**もん** is a shorter, more casual version of もの. However, it carries a significantly more childish or cute nuance and should generally be avoided in regular conversation unless you intend to sound that way.

**Important Considerations:**

*   もの is considered casual, so it's best to avoid it in formal situations.
*   The nuance of もの suggests a slightly self-justifying or explanatory tone.
