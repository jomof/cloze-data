Think of it as an abbreviated form of **そうすると**. It often appears at the beginning of a new sentence to emphasize the consequence of the preceding statement.

Here's a breakdown of its nuances:

*   **Sequential Occurrence:**  It signifies that B happened *after* A.
*   **Natural Consequence:** The outcome in the second clause is often something that naturally follows from the first, or something the speaker has no control over.
*   **Confirmation/Conclusion:**  When used in response to someone else's statement, **すると** can indicate that the speaker is drawing a logical conclusion or seeking confirmation of their understanding, similar to saying "so you mean...?" or "if that's the case...".

**Important Considerations:**

*   The "result" indicated by **すると** is typically something outside the speaker's direct control or a conclusion they are drawing.
*   When used to confirm understanding, the nuance leans towards "if so, then..." or "so you're saying...".
